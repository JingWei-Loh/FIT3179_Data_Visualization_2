Covid-19

The coronavirus, also known as COVID-19, is a highly contagious viral infection caused by the severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2). It was first identified in Wuhan, China, in December 2019 and has since spread globally, leading to a pandemic.

Click to highlight interested country

In 2023, Magnolia, Australia, Austria, France, Slavonia, Germany, and Italy are among the countries with the highest COVID-19 active cases due to high population densities and international connectivity (tourism).

Antarctica and Turkmenistan have the fewest COVID-19 active cases. Antarctica's lack of permanent human population have led to the lowest case numbers

Policy Responses: Stay-at-Home Restriction

Proportion of Countries with Various COVID-19 Policy Responses in Year 2020, 2021 and 2022

In 2020 and 2021, the majority of countries implemented stay-at-home policies. and significant number of countries recommend citizen to stay at home without mandatory regulations.

By 2022, the COVID-19 situation improved with widespread vaccination, leading many countries to adopt a No-measures policy

Deaths due to Covid-19 by Continent

COVID-19 Deaths Compared Across Continents

South America has the highest COVID-19 death rate at approximately 38 deaths per 10,000 population, followed by North America with around 27 deaths per 10,000 population.

In contrast, Africa reports the lowest death rate at just 2 per 10,000 population.
